    Background

    NCT 127 is the second sub-group of NCT, a South Korean K-Pop boy band under the entertainment company SM Entertainment in 2016. The music produced by 127 is a style of noise music unique to their group in the K-pop industry. For this reason, NCT 127 has achieved worldwide success and been recognised as a trailblazer of musical style in the K-pop industry. The group’s music often conveys more mature concepts and experimental sound compared to their younger counterparts, NCT Dream.

    Group Dynamic

    As of 2021, NCT 127 has 9 members: Taeil, Johnny, Yuta, Taeyong, Doyoung, Jaehyun, Jungwoo, Mark and Haechan. Originally, the group had 7 members, Taeil, Taeyong, Yuta, Jaehyun, Winwin, Mark, and Haechan, with Doyoung and Johnny joining in December 2016. Jungwoo joined the group in 2018. Winwin no longer participates in 127, even though his departure was never officially announced.

    Achievements

    NCT 127 has been globally recognised as a K-pop group with a unique sound, breaking into the K-pop mainstream with their song “Cherry Bomb” (2017). Further international recognition was achieved with the release of their first studio album and lead single “Regular” in 2018. This global popularity prompted SM Entertainment to sign deals with Capitol Music Group, acknowledging NCT 127’s global impact.

    Album Success

    The September 2021 release of NCT 127’s third studio album “Sticker” in 2021 earnt the group the title of ‘double million sellers’, selling over 2,000,000 pre-order copies. On the first day of availability, the album had already surpassed 1 million preorders. The rising success of this album has led to multiple global interactions and events, such as appearances on American late-night shows and collaborations with the American market.
